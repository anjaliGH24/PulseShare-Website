How to Donate Blood?

Step 1:
Register

Begin by registering as a blood donor, providing essential personal information such as your name, contact details, age, weight, and blood group. This step helps create your donor profile and allows the blood donation center to keep track of your donation history.

Step 2:
Medical History

After registration, you’ll undergo a medical history assessment where you’ll be asked questions about your health, recent travel, medications, and any past illnesses or surgeries. This assessment ensures that you meet the eligibility criteria for blood donation and helps identify any potential risks or concerns.

Step 3:
Donate Blood

Once your medical history is reviewed and approved, you’ll proceed to the donation process. A trained healthcare professional will clean the donation site on your arm, insert a sterile needle to collect blood, and monitor the process to ensure your comfort and safety. The donation typically takes about 10-15 minutes, after which you’ll be provided with refreshments and rest.

Step 4:
Refreshment

After donating blood, take a moment to relax and enjoy some refreshments provided by the donation center. This brief period of rest allows your body to recover and ensures you feel rejuvenated before leaving. Your comfort and well-being are our priorities, and our team is here to support you throughout the donation process.
